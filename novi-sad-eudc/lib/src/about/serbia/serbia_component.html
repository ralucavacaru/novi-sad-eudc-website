Serbia Cheat Sheet

Welcome to Serbia! Country of endless gastronomic, oenologic, cultural and historical possibilities! Super successful debate, and “regular” history aside, it is our duty to prepare you for a week here! That said, here you can find all the necessary information that might help you pack and plan.

Population of Serbia: Over 7 million

Population of Novi Sad: 286,546

Official language: Serbian (However, most of the people will understand English!)

Currency: Serbian Dinar (1 Euro = 118 RSD ; 1 USD = 102 RSD)

Meals

Depending on what you eat. If you wish to try out Serbian street “fast food” cuisine which offers variety of meat, pita’s, and a famous type of Index sandwich (sandwich symbol of Novi Sad) - Plan on spending 300 RSD. If you wish to have a sit down meal in a nice restaurant, average price is around 600 RSD. If there’s two of you, and wish to have a 3 course meal, with a bottle of wine, the price will be around 3000 RSD.

Cabs

From one part of Novi Sad, to another, the cab should not exceed 700 RSD

Drinks in bars

  • Around 150 RSD for non-alcoholic beverages
  • 150 RSD for 0.5 draught beer,
  • 120 RSD for a shot of rakija,
  • Around 1200 RSD for a bottle of wine (also depends on which wine do you get - Prices can go up to 4000 RSD, since Serbia is a wine producing country and options are always available in bars and restaurants)

Basic Serbian words and phrases:

Hello! = Zdravo

Yes = Da

No = Ne

Please = Molim Vas

I don’t understand = Ne razumem

I don’t know = Ne znam

Do you speak English? = Da li govorite Engleski?

Excuse me! = Izvinite!

Thank you = Hvala

How are you? = Three options, depending who do you ask

  • Kako si? - If you are asking someone of your age, or younger
  • Kako ste? - There are two or more people you are asking
  • Kako ste Vi? - Formal, or asking someone older than yourself

What’s your name? = Kako se zovesh?

My name is… = Zovem se…

I’m from… = Ja sam iz (name of the country)

Goodbye = Dovidjenja / Vidimo se kasnije! ( See you later!)
